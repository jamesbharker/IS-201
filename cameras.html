Which Camera Should I Buy?



An Uncomprehensive List of the Best Camera Options on the Market






Budget



Here's where it gets real. Cameras can cost anywhere from $30 to $3,000,000, so figuring out how much you can spend is imperative. Just remember that buying a camera is a lot more than just the camera.


Make sure you factor in how much you'll need for any rigging necessary to make the camera work. That will probably include an external monitor, handles, rods, follow-focus units, batteries, cables, and a good fluid-head tripod that can support the weight of the rig.

My Recommendation



Always go with the Arri if you can. Their color is unparalled and it's the best possible image you can get. If you need a dual-native ISO and you want to down-sample high-quality footage, go with the Venice II.


If you don't have $70k to spend on a camera, then you should get Red. Their RED RAW is phenomenal and gets you pretty close to Arri and the Sony Venice. If you can't fork over the $30k or $10k, then stick with the Sony FX3. It's very good for the price.
